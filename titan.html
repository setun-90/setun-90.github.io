Titan

Don't do your runtime's job, let your runtime do your job for you

Synopsis

Titan is a macro language for optimisable incremental search. In Titan, programs reduce terms to their normal forms according to the theory of graph reduction. For this, Titan defines:

  1. A reference language in which to express terms, including programs;
  2. A standard library by which to facilitate development; and
  3. Implementation constraints to provide performance guarantees.

Reference Language

Titan's reference language is extremely simple: it is just a preorder traversal of a term graph. The caveat is that Titan also allows partial application (per the requirement of incremental search), so parentheses are required if a function must be partially applied.

Standard Library

Titan's standard library is divided into three major categories: Environment, Language, and Algorithms.
